# 3D-Printed Toy Car with Planetary-Gear Wheels

<figure>
  <img src="assets/images/Planetary_Gear_Car/planetary-gear-car.jpg" alt="Image of the 3D printed toy car with planetary-gear wheels.">
</figure>

I had the idea to make a toy car with planetary gear wheels after seeing [Emmett Lalish's Gear Bearing on Thingiverse](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:53451).
Planetary gears are excellent candidates for 3D-printed bearings (or wheels) because they have low friction.

There were two primary design challenges for this project:
1. Design the complex body of the vehicle in SOLIDWORKS.
2. Enable printing and assembly of each gear seperately (as opposed to print-in-place) to allow multiple colors (black for the tire, gold/copper for the spokes, and silver for the hub cap).

## Testing and Revisions
Unfortunately, the first time a kid threw the toy, one of the wheels snapped off.
This is because the initial design had a stress concentrator where the wheel shaft attached to the vehicle body.
In the next iteration, I modified my design to include an embedded steel shaft to address this issue.
